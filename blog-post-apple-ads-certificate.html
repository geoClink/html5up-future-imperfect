Completing the Apple Ads Certificate: Key Rules & Strategies for iOS App Store Advertising

App Store Advertising | Mar 21, 2026

On March 21, 2026, I completed the Apple Search Ads Certification, deepening my understanding of how to effectively promote apps on the iOS App Store. Here are the most important rules and strategies I learned for advertising applications and maximizing visibility, downloads, and ROI:

Essential Rules for App Store Advertising

  • Follow Apple’s Ad Guidelines: All ads must comply with Apple’s creative and content policies. Avoid misleading claims, prohibited content, and ensure your app’s metadata is accurate and up to date.
  • Respect User Privacy: Use Apple’s privacy-friendly attribution and never collect or use user data for targeting without consent. Leverage SKAdNetwork for privacy-compliant campaign measurement.
  • Maintain High App Quality: Only advertise apps that meet Apple’s quality standards—good ratings, no crashes, and up-to-date with the latest iOS requirements.
  • Be Transparent: Clearly communicate your app’s value proposition in ad copy and screenshots. Avoid clickbait or deceptive practices.

Effective Strategies for iOS App Store Ads

  • Keyword Optimization: Research and bid on relevant keywords that match your app’s core features and user intent. Use Apple’s keyword suggestion tools and monitor performance regularly.
  • Creative Testing: Continuously A/B test ad creatives—icons, screenshots, and copy—to find what resonates best with your target audience.
  • Audience Targeting: Use Apple’s audience refinement tools to target users by location, device type, demographics, and previous app interactions.
  • Budget Management: Set clear daily and total campaign budgets. Monitor cost-per-tap (CPT) and cost-per-acquisition (CPA) to optimize spend for the best ROI.
  • Leverage Custom Product Pages: Create tailored App Store pages for different audiences or campaigns to improve conversion rates.
  • Track & Analyze Results: Use Apple Search Ads dashboard and SKAdNetwork data to measure installs, engagement, and optimize campaigns based on real performance data.

Keyword Match Types & Bidding

In Apple Search Ads, the match type you choose for a keyword determines how closely a user’s search must match your keyword for your ad to appear. Exact match gives you the most control and typically warrants a higher bid, since you know the term is highly relevant. Broad match and Search Match cast a wider net, so you may want to start with lower bids and adjust as you see which terms perform best. As a rule of thumb: bid more aggressively on exact match keywords you know convert, and use more conservative bids for broad or discovery-focused campaigns until you identify new high-performers.

Keyword Mining (Harvesting)

In Apple Search Ads, “keyword mining” (sometimes called “harvesting”) is the process of using Discovery campaigns and Search Match to uncover new, high-performing search terms you might not have thought of. When these new keywords start driving installs or conversions, you move them into your Exact Match campaigns and add them as negatives in Discovery. This keeps your data clean and ensures you’re always expanding your reach with fresh, relevant keywords. It’s a continuous cycle: discover, test, promote, and repeat.

Structuring Search Results Campaigns

The first step in structuring search results campaigns is to divide your keywords into four separate campaigns, each focused on a different keyword theme. This structure helps you measure results, monitor performance, optimize campaigns, and scale for long-term growth. The four recommended campaign types are:

  • Brand: Target keywords related to your app or company name. These capture users already searching for you and help protect your brand presence.
  • Category: Focus on generic keywords that describe your app’s function or category (e.g., “fitness tracker,” “budget planner”). Great for reaching users interested in your app’s space.
  • Competitor: Bid on keywords related to competing apps. This can help you attract users considering alternatives, but requires careful monitoring and compliance with Apple’s policies.
  • Discovery: Use broad match and search match to uncover new, high-performing keywords you may not have considered. This campaign is for exploration and growth.

Note: If you already have existing search campaigns, avoid importing them directly into Apple.
